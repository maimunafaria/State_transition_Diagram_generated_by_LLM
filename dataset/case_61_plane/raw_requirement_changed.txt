When a plane is first acquired, it is placed on order and remains in that stage until it is delivered. Once the plane arrives, it is prepared so that it can be used for flights.

The plane is then used for regular operations. After completing a flight, it may either be assigned to another flight or sent for maintenance, depending on its condition and schedule.

If the plane still has more flights to operate, it continues being used. But when there are no more flights scheduled, or if maintenance is required, it is sent for maintenance.

During maintenance, the plane is checked and serviced. Once it is ready again, it becomes available for use and can return to normal operations.

If at any point the plane is no longer available for use, it is taken out of service. In some cases, the plane may be permanently withdrawn, after which it is no longer part of the system.
