In many systems, certain rates or values are only valid for a specific period of time. Managing these rates manually can lead to confusion, especially when it is unclear whether a rate is currently valid, not yet started, or already expired.

To solve this, the system manages the lifecycle of a rate based on time.

When a new rate is created, it is not immediately applied. Instead, it remains in a waiting phase until its start date is reached. This ensures that rates are only used when they are meant to become effective.

Once the current date reaches or passes the defined start date, the rate becomes active and can be used by the system.

The rate continues to remain active until its validity period ends. When the finish date is reached, the rate is no longer valid and is considered expired.

After a certain period of time, such as one year from its expiration, the system automatically removes or archives the rate so that outdated information does not remain in use.

This approach ensures that rates are applied only within their valid time period, reducing errors and maintaining consistency in the system.
