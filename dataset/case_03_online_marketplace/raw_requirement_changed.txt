A person uses an online marketplace either as a seller or as a customer.

If the person joins as a seller, they first create an account by providing their details. After completing registration and verification, the seller signs in and enters their workspace.

If the person joins as a customer, they sign in and access their personal dashboard.

On the seller side, the seller can carry out different activities. They can add new products, promote products that are already listed, or communicate with customers. After completing any of these activities, the seller continues using the platform from their workspace. The seller can also choose to leave the platform at any time.

On the customer side, the customer can explore products, communicate with sellers, report a seller, or share feedback about products.

When browsing products, the customer searches and views available items. After viewing, they may either stop browsing or choose to continue with a purchase. If they decide to continue, the selected product is added and they proceed to checkout. During checkout, payment is handled. After the purchase attempt, whether it succeeds or not, the customer continues using the platform.

The customer can also communicate with a seller and then continue browsing or using other features. If needed, the customer can report a seller based on previous interactions. They can also rate and review a product after receiving it, and then continue using the platform.

At any point, both the seller and the customer can choose to leave the platform, after which they are logged out.
