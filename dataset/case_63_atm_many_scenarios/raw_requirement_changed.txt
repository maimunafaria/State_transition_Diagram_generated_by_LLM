The ATM statechart starts in the Idle state. Upon a customer inserting their card and entering a PIN, followed by selecting cash withdrawal, the ATM transitions through several states based on specific events:

1. **Card Inserted Event**: Transitions from Idle to Waiting for PIN.
2. **PIN Entered Event**: Transitions from Waiting for PIN to Validating PIN.
3. **Valid PIN Event**: Transitions from Validating PIN to Waiting for Customer Choice, where the customer can choose to withdraw cash or cancel.

In the Validating PIN state:
- If the PIN is correct (Valid PIN event), transitions to Waiting for Customer Choice.
- If the PIN is incorrect and the attempt count is less than three (Invalid PIN event), returns to Waiting for PIN.
- If the third attempt fails, transitions to Confiscating due to a reported lost or stolen card.

In the Waiting for Customer Choice state:
- A Cancel event in any of these states results in entering the Ejecting state and terminating the transaction.
- Selecting withdrawal leads to the Withdrawal Selected event, transitioning to Processing Withdrawal.
4. **Withdrawal Approved**: Transitions from Processing Withdrawal to Dispensing where cash is dispensed.
5. **Cash Dispensed Event**: Transitions to Printing for receipt printing.
6. **Receipt Printed**: Transitions to Ejecting as the card is ejected.
7. **Card Ejected Event**: Ends in the Terminating state.

The diagram also shows that some events, like PIN Entered in Idle, are ignored, and multiple transitions can occur from a single state based on different events.
