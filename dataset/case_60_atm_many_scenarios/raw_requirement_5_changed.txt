A user reaches the ATM and proceeds to choose the withdrawal option. The machine begins processing the request and checks with the system to see if the transaction can be approved.

If everything is fine, the ATM dispenses the cash. After giving the money, it prints a receipt, returns the card, and completes the session. After a short while, the machine resets and becomes ready for the next user.

However, things do not always go smoothly. If the withdrawal request is rejected during processing, the ATM immediately cancels the operation and returns the card.

Even after approval, another issue may occur. If the machine does not have enough cash, it cannot complete the transaction. In that case, it stops the operation, shows that the system is unavailable, and ends the session.
