A user, such as an inventory manager or salesperson, accesses the system by signing in using their credentials. After accessing the system, the user can perform various operations related to managing inventory and handling sales activities.

The user can add new goods along with their details to the inventory, update the price of existing items, or remove goods that are no longer required. The system also identifies expired goods and notifies about their removal from the inventory.

When required, the user can place an order to a supplier for additional goods. Once the order is placed, an invoice is generated for that order.

For customer transactions, the user searches for items available in the inventory and selects them for purchase. Based on the selected items, the system calculates the applicable discount and tax. After that, a receipt is generated for the customer.

During the billing process, the user may replace an item or cancel the receipt if needed.

The system also records customer requests for items that may not be currently available.

At any point, the user can view a summary report that includes details of all transactions carried out in the system.

If required, the user can update their account password.
