For Online College Magazine System — Polished Requirement Specification

Overview
College magazines provide an authoritative, recognizable and artistic platform of expression to the student body. However, paperback college magazines narrow the scope for a more extensive audience and are lavish of important resources like paper, especially when the magazines are printed seasonally on campus year after year. Consequently, many students may not be able to make it to the print media of the college.

Operational Context
Primary actors include student.

Functional Requirements
1. The system shall enable students to liberally generate content for and about the college.
2. The system shall allow anyone in any part of the world to read articles being published in the online magazine.
3. The system shall have minimal resource usage while providing an experience as efficient as in paperback magazines.
4. The system shall allow users to read articles.
5. The system shall enable users to write articles.
6. The system shall allow users to draft articles.
7. The system shall enable users to edit and publish articles.
8. The system shall allow selected students and faculty to regularly write and post articles for the magazine.
9. The system shall enable contributors (like alumni) to write for the magazine occasionally.
10. The system shall allow guests or readers to read, comment on, and rate articles.
11. The system shall enable guests to act as contributors if they are interested.
12. The system shall require moderators to review articles before publishing to decide their pertinence.
13. The system shall organize articles based on categories appropriate to the workflow of the college.
