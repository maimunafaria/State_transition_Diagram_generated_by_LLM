Cash Register — Polished Requirement Specification

Overview
This exercise concerns a simplified system of a supermarket cash register. It is inspired to a great extent by the case study of C. Larman’s first book (the Point-ofSale System), which formed the basis of the Valtech training about OOAD.

Operational Context
Primary actors include customer.

Functional Requirements
1. The system shall support this exercise concerns a simplified system of a supermarket cash register.
2. The system shall ensure that it is inspired to a great extent by the case study of C.
3. The system shall ensure that the standard procedure of using a cash register is as follows: • A customer arrives at the checkout to pay for various items • The cashier records the bar code number of each item, as well as the quantity if it is greater than one.
4. The system shall support • The cash register displays the price of each item and its description.
5. The system shall support • The cash register displays the total cost of the purchases.
6. The system shall ensure that • The customer selects his or her payment method: • Cash: the cashier takes the money from the customer and puts it in the cash register, the cash register indicates how much change the customer is to be given; • Cheque: the cashier verifies that the customer is financially solvent by sending a request to an authorisation centre via the cash register; • Credit card: a banking terminal forms part of the cash register.
7. The system shall support it sends a request for authorisation to an authorisation centre, according to the card type.
8. The system shall support • The cash register records the sale and prints a receipt.
9. The system shall support • The cashier gives the receipt to the customer.
10. The system shall support once the items have been entered, the customer can present money-off vouchers for certain items to the cashier.
11. The system shall ensure that when the payment transaction is finished, the cash register sends the information on the number of items sold to the stock management system.
