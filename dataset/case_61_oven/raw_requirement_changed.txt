A microwave oven works based on whether the door is open or closed and whether food is inside.

When the door is closed, the user can open it at any time. When the door is open, the user can place food inside or remove it.

After placing food inside, the user closes the door. Then, the user enters the cooking time. Once the time is set, the microwave is ready to start.

When the user starts the microwave, it begins cooking the food. It continues cooking until the set time is finished.

If the door is opened while cooking, the microwave stops immediately for safety.

When the cooking time is over, the microwave stops, and the user can open the door and take the food out.
