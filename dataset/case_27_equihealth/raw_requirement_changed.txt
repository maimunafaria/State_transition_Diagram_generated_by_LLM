A person first enters the platform. If the person is new, they create an account. If the registration is not completed, it ends there. Otherwise, they continue to sign in.

After signing in, the person may enter as a patient, a health worker, or a government official.

If the person enters as a patient, the patient dashboard is shown. From there, the patient can provide medical information. After the information is entered, it can be checked by a health worker, and it can also be used for further analysis.

If the information is analyzed, it may then be used to prepare alerts, plan activities for health workers, and support health planning.

Alerts may be sent back to the patient or to the health worker. Planned activities are shown to the health worker. Health planning results are shown to the government official.

If the person enters as a health worker, the health worker dashboard is shown, and the health worker can also check the medical information provided earlier.

If the person enters as a government official, the government dashboard is shown for related work.

After finishing what they need to do, the person logs out, and it ends.
