E Ration Shop — Polished Requirement Specification

Overview
Public Distribution System (PDS) is an Indian food security system. It is established by the Government of India under the Ministry of Consumer Affairs, Food, and Public Distribution and managed jointly with state governments in India. The traditional PDS is used to distribute grocery items to India’s poor who are valid ration cardholders.

Operational Context
Primary actors include admin, users.

Functional Requirements
1. The system shall allow ration cardholders to place orders for grocery items online.
2. The system shall make the distribution process fully automated and computerized.
3. The system shall remove the drawbacks associated with issuing products based on ration cards.
4. The system shall display the geo-location of shops on a map for easy identification.
5. The system shall provide detailed information about shops, shopkeepers, ration cards, and regions.
6. The system shall display the opening and closing hours of shops for users' reference.
7. The system shall enable retailers to manage their shop's items by adding, editing, deleting, and updating them.
8. The system shall allow retailers to advertise their shops for increased visibility.
9. The system shall notify users about the availability of stocks on a regular basis.
10. The system shall implement a product delivery feature for convenient customer experience.
11. The system shall provide a complaint forum where users can raise their concerns and issues.
12. The system shall integrate an easy payment system using PayTM, Cash on Delivery (COD), and other convenient options.
13. The system shall implement administrative and moderation features for the government and shopkeepers to manage their accounts effectively.
14. The system shall implement a secure authentication system using Aadhaar numbers and One-Time Pins (OTPs) for user verification.
