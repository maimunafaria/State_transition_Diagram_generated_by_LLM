A person opens the app and either creates an account or signs in.

If they are creating an account, they enter their details. If everything is correct, they can then sign in. If something is wrong, they won’t be able to continue.

When signing in, they enter their username and password. If the details don’t match, they won’t be able to log in. If everything is correct, they can start using the app.

After logging in, they have several options.

They can read blogs shared by recovered patients and then come back to continue using the app.

They can update the number of infected people in their area. After that, they can check how many people are infected, how many deaths have happened, or how many masks are needed. If they check the mask demand, shops prepare masks and gloves.

A person who is in home quarantine can share their information. A doctor can view that information and, if needed, suggest medicines. The person can then see what the doctor shared and decide whether to buy medicines.

They can also view a list of nearby medical shops and buy medicines if needed.

Another option is assigning workers to clean an area. After that, the cleanliness status of that area is updated.

Once they are done with these activities, such as buying medicines, stocking supplies, or updating cleanliness, they stop using the app.
