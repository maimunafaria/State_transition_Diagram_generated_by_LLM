After the user finishes interacting with the ATM—whether they completed a transaction, cancelled it, or something went wrong—the machine begins to wrap things up.

If the card is invalid, expired, or the PIN was entered incorrectly too many times, the ATM keeps the card and informs the user.

If the user cancels the operation or the request is rejected, the machine simply returns the card with a message.

If the transaction is successful, the ATM may first print a receipt and, if needed, dispense cash. After completing these actions, it returns the card to the user.

Once the card is ejected, the system considers the session finished. It then moves into a short ending phase before resetting itself.

Finally, depending on the situation, the ATM either goes back to its normal waiting condition or shuts down if required.
