In an online shopping system, users can browse products through a catalog. When they enter the catalog, they can view items directly or explore different ways of browsing.

Users can look through products using an index, either by browsing alphabetically or by category. They can switch between these options depending on how they prefer to search. Once they find something interesting, they can select a product to view its details.

At any time, the user can choose to go to their basket to review the items they have selected. From there, they can either return to the catalog to continue browsing or move forward to checkout.

During checkout, the user reviews their selections and proceeds with the purchase. If needed, they can still go back to the catalog to make changes before completing the process.

This setup allows users to smoothly move between browsing products, viewing details, managing their basket, and completing their purchase in a flexible and user-friendly way.
