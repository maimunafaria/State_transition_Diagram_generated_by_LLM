Research Methodology 
 
3.1 Introduction 
This chapter presents the research methodology for investigating the automatic generation of 
UML State Transition Diagrams (STDs) from natural language requirements using Large 
Language Models (LLMs). The study adopts a comparative and modeling-oriented experimental 
design. 
Unlike purely NLP-focused evaluations, this research prioritizes structural correctness, 
behavioral completeness, and modeling consistency. The methodology integrates structured 
prompting, retrieval augmentation, graph-based validation, and an ensemble refinement 
mechanism to improve reliability. 
The study evaluates whether smaller open-source LLMs, when enhanced with engineering 
techniques, can approach or surpass a proprietary GPT-4o baseline in behavioral modeling 
tasks. 
 
3.2 Problem Description 
3.2.1 Research Objective 
The objective of this research is twofold: 
1.‚Äã To compare the performance of proprietary and open-source LLMs in generating state 
transition diagrams from natural language requirements. 
2.‚Äã To design and evaluate a reliability-enhanced framework that improves modeling 
correctness, structural validity, and stability in open-source LLM outputs. 
The study aims to determine whether structured prompting and validation techniques can 
compensate for model size differences in behavioral modeling tasks.

The research focuses exclusively on:

‚óè‚Äã Structured JSON output

3.12 Summary 
This methodology establishes a reproducible and modeling-focused framework for evaluating 
LLM-driven generation of UML state transition diagrams. The experimental design integrates 
model comparison, structured reliability mechanisms, and ablation analysis to determine 
whether engineering techniques can enable smaller open-source models to achieve competitive 
behavioral modeling performance relative to a proprietary baseline.
