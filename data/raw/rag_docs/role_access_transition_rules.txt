Title: Role-Based Access and Transition Rules

Purpose:
Encode who can trigger which transitions to prevent invalid control flow.

States:
- Request Created
- Waiting Approval
- Approved
- Rejected
- Executed
- Cancelled

Events and role mapping:
- create_request (employee, customer)
- submit_for_approval (request_owner)
- approve (manager, admin)
- reject (manager, admin)
- execute (system, operator)
- cancel (request_owner, admin)

Required transitions:
- RequestCreated --> WaitingApproval before managerial decision.
- WaitingApproval --> Approved or Rejected by authorized role.
- Approved --> Executed by system or operator.

Forbidden transitions:
- Do not allow employee/customer to trigger approve directly.
- Do not allow execute from WaitingApproval.
- Restrict cancel after Executed unless reversal path exists.

Terminal states:
- Executed and Cancelled are typical terminal outcomes.
- Rejected may be terminal or returnable to RequestCreated.

PlantUML mini example:
@startuml
[*] --> RequestCreated
RequestCreated --> WaitingApproval : submit_for_approval
WaitingApproval --> Approved : approve_by_manager
WaitingApproval --> Rejected : reject_by_manager
Approved --> Executed : execute
RequestCreated --> Cancelled : cancel_by_owner
Executed --> [*]
Cancelled --> [*]
@enduml
